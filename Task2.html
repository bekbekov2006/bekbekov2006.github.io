Tribute to Isaac Newton

About Isaac Newton

Sir Isaac Newton (1642-1727) was an English mathematician, physicist, astronomer, and author who is widely recognized as one of the most influential scientists of all time. He formulated the laws of motion and universal gravitation, laying the foundation for classical mechanics. His work in optics and mathematics also changed the course of science forever.

Timeline

  1. 1642 - Born in Woolsthorpe, England
  2. 1661 - Admitted to Trinity College, Cambridge
  3. 1687 - Published Philosophiæ Naturalis Principia Mathematica
  4. 1696 - Appointed Warden of the Royal Mint
  5. 1703 - Elected President of the Royal Society
  6. 1727 - Died in London, buried in Westminster Abbey

Key Contributions

  • Formulated the laws of motion and universal gravitation
  • Developed calculus (independently alongside Leibniz)
  • Made groundbreaking discoveries in optics
  • Designed the reflecting telescope
